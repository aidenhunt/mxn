About Mariani, Jorhat

Last edited on 6 December 2020, at 05:38 (UTC). Wiki

Mariani is a neighbourhood town of Jorhat. It is about 17.5 km from Jorhat Town railway station. Mariani is in the border of Nagaland. Mariani is famous for the Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary which is located on the roadside on the way to Nakachari from Mariani at a distance of 5 km. Pincode of Mariani is 785634.

Overview

Mariani is a Town Committee city in district of Jorhat, Assam. The Mariani city is divided into 5 wards for which elections are held every 5 years. The Mariani Town Committee has population of 20,801 of which 10,781 are males while 10,020 are females as per report released by Census India 2011.Data from Population Census 2011

Population of Children with age of 0-6 is 1849 which is 8.89 % of total population of Mariani (TC). In Mariani Town Committee, Female Sex Ratio is of 929 against state average of 958. Moreover Child Sex Ratio in Mariani is around 965 compared to Assam state average of 962. Literacy rate of Mariani city is 89.91 % higher than state average of 72.19 %. In Mariani, Male literacy is around 92.80 % while female literacy rate is 86.79 % .

Mariani Town Committee has total administration over 4,684 houses to which it supplies basic amenities like water and sewerage. It is also authorize to build roads within Town Committee limits and impose taxes on properties coming under its jurisdiction.

Mariani Caste Factor

Schedule Caste (SC) constitutes 4.78 % while Schedule Tribe (ST) were 1.31 % of total population in Mariani (TC).

Mariani Work Profile

Out of total population, 7,549 were engaged in work or business activity. Of this 6,588 were males while 961 were females. In census survey, worker is defined as person who does business, job, service, and cultivator and labour activity. Of total 7549 working population, 87.65 % were engaged in Main Work while 12.35 % of total workers were engaged in Marginal Work.

Hoollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary

The Hoollongapar Gibbon Sanctuary, formerly known as the Gibbon Wildlife Sanctuary or Hollongapar Reserve Forest, is an isolated protected area of evergreen forest located in Assam, India. The sanctuary was officially constituted and renamed in 1997.
